Skeleton Studio

The building
has a voice now.

A construction intelligence agent. Delivered on hardware. Grounded in knowledge graphs. Persistent for the life of the building.

What's inside
I've been building houses on the South African coast for 25 years. Every one of them goes silent at handover. The drawings go in a folder. The knowledge walks out the door with whoever built it.
What's in the box

Five layers.
One persistent intelligence.

Not a dashboard. Not a SaaS login. A physical object with a building's entire technical knowledge encoded inside it. Each layer does one thing and depends on the layer beneath.

01
Hardware
Mac Mini M4. Always on. Local. No subscription. No cloud dependency. No data leaves the building. It enters the asset register at handover and outlasts the management company, the contractor, and the architect's phone number.
02
The Graph
A labeled property graph of the entire project in Neo4j. Not a document store. Not vector embeddings. Explicit, traversable relationships.
WALL → CONNECTS_TO → FOUNDATION ROOM → ON_LEVEL → 2
The building's logic, not just its geometry.
03
The Enrichment
This is the craft layer. A Revit export is geometry. An enriched graph is knowledge. Specifications. Material decisions. Construction sequencing. The context that lives in emails, site meetings, WhatsApp threads. A generative AI cannot do this. It requires someone who has built 500 buildings and knows what matters when it's raining on site at 7am.
04
The Soul
The agent's identity, constraints, and authority boundaries. What it is allowed to know. How it learns without being poisoned. Supervised enrichment — only authorised inputs update the graph. The builders don't teach it. The knowledge grows in one direction: toward accuracy.
05
The Intelligence
Not a chatbot. It doesn't wait to be asked. It monitors. It flags. It mirrors commitments back without emotion. When it answers, it answers from the graph — not from training data, not from a hallucination. From the actual building. GraphRAG: the answer is in the database or it isn't given.
The problem

The Illusion Gap.

AI has transformed how architecture is imagined. It has not changed how buildings are built. There is a gap between what AI produces and what construction actually needs. Nobody has named it. Nobody has closed it.

What AI gives architecture
Spectacular imagery. Unbuilable.
The most advanced offices in the world prompt Midjourney. They generate images that have never touched a structural engineer. The renders are extraordinary. The knowledge to construct them is absent. This is not a criticism. It is a gap.
What construction actually needs
Queryable knowledge. Grounded truth.
What level is the garage on? Which walls are loadbearing? What did the engineer specify for that connection? Who committed to being on site Wednesday? These are not design questions. They require answers from the building itself.

Research context

The published research maps exactly where this work sits — and exactly what hasn't been done yet. MaCAD is where the gap gets formalised, extended, and published.

BIMConverse Thesis · IAAC · 2024
Pacheco & Berkmiller established a Revit → IFC → Neo4j pipeline with natural language querying. Their stated future work: "CI/CD pipeline integration via webhooks for files hosted in Speckle; expanded graph schemas; multi-project knowledge graph federation." The Speckle → Neo4j pathway was named as aspirational. One forum post asked if it was possible. No one had built it.
Current state · March 2026
The Speckle → Neo4j pipeline exists. It runs on real practice data. 730 Revit elements, extracted via Speckle, mapped to 4,318 graph nodes with 1,264 explicit relationships. Multi-project federation and supervised construction-phase enrichment: unexplored in published research. Alan Rynne from Speckle is on MaCAD faculty. This is the research gap directly in front of the program.
